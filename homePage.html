Murray Gell-Mann

Smaller than an Atom

Murray Gell-Mann is best known for his pre-eminent role in the discovery and conceptualisation of elementary particles. Elementary particles (coined as “Quarks” by Gell-Mann) are the fundamental building blocks of the subatomic particles that we know of. Along with this discovery, Gell-Mann played a key role in developing the concept of chirality in weak nuclear interactions.

1929 - 2019

Murray Gell-Mann, born on September 15, 1929, in New York, US. He passed away on May 24, 2019 in New Mexico, US.

1969 Nobel Prize

In 1969 Murray Gell-Mann was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for “his contributions and discoveries concerning the classification of elementary particles and their interactions”
