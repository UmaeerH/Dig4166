New York City, 1929

Born in September of 1929, Gell-Mann's birth had followed the great crash of 29. He was born into The Great Depression.

Education

Early Education

Murray Gell-Mann attended Columbia Grammar & Preparatory School where he graduated at the age of 14 as the Valedictorian

Post Grad

Gell-Mann received his Ph.D. in Physics from MIT in 1951.His doctoral dissertation was titled "Coupling strength and nuclear reactions"

University

Gell-Mann earned a Bachelor degree in Physics from Yale University, located in Connecticut

Yale University is an Ivy League university, seen as prestigious and desirable. Gell-Mann sought to remain in an Ivy League University for his post-grad education and applied to Princeton and Harvard. Being rejected by Princeton and unable to get financial support for Harvard, Murray Gell-Mann went to Massachusetts Institute of Technology

Personal Life

Gell-Mann married J. Margaret Dow in 1955, having a son and daughter with her. Margaret passed away in 1981, and Gell-Mann married Marcia Southwick in 1992

Gell-Mann had many other passions, often being described as a polymath for his vast interests and knowledge. Some of these include Archaeology, Linguistics and Numismatics

MIT or Suicide

After being rejected from Princeton and not being able to attend Harvard due to financial hardship, Gell-Mann had wrote about being miserable with having to attend MIT. This was prior to MIT's world renowned reputation in Physics.

Murray contemplated suicide and decided to attend MIT under the impression that he could always commit suicide after witnessing the University for himself


Murray Gell-Mann passed away at the age of 89 on May 24, 2019, at his home in New Mexico. He was often described as being well versed in many languages and topics, once being called the living "Encyclopedia Britannica"
